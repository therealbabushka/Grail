## Design Context

### Users
Grail is built for Counter-Strike 2 skin traders, collectors, and hunters using the app as a daily “command center” to:
- track real profit/loss and capital at risk (trading-desk mindset),
- curate and share beautiful CT/T loadouts (collector/gallery mindset),
- maintain a target watchlist with price/float constraints and fast marketplace jumps (hunter/HUD mindset).

The interface should feel **calm, premium, and confidence-inducing**—a cockpit you can trust while making quick decisions.

### Brand Personality
**Tactical · Premium · Precise**

Voice/tone: concise, high-signal, never cute; uses trading/HUD language (“positions”, “targets”, “acquired”, “at risk”) without feeling like a game overlay.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: dark-first (supports light tokens, but default experience is dark).
- **Visual tone**: “dark trading cockpit” with crisp typography, controlled contrast, and selective glow.
- **References**: Apple-level polish (spacing, hierarchy, finish), but with a tougher tactical/terminal edge.
- **Anti-references**: avoid generic pastel SaaS dashboards; avoid Material/Google-y styling; avoid ultra-minimal monochrome that removes personality.
- **Motion**: bold and purposeful (rarity glow, 3D tilt, acquired stamp), always respecting reduced-motion where applicable.

### Design Principles
1. **Signal over decoration**: every visual flourish must reinforce state (profit/loss, rarity, status, focus), not add noise.
2. **Premium restraint**: sparse, intentional layouts; strong typographic hierarchy; micro-details that feel crafted.
3. **Tactical clarity**: fast scanning patterns (labels in small caps/mono, numeric emphasis, predictable placement).
4. **Semantic color discipline**: use dedicated tokens (profit/loss/warning/info/rarity) consistently; never rely on color alone.
5. **Accessible by default**: target WCAG 2.2 AA; keep focus-visible strong; support reduced motion and legible contrast.
